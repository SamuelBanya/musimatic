Background

This is a webpage that describes the background of the country, Monaco.

Time Periods

1400-1800

Monaco was ruled by the Grimaldi family who had purchased the country in 1419. Family members then intermarried with French and Italian nobles until 1793 when French Revolutionary forces captured Monaco until 1814 when the Grimaldi family returned to the throne.

19th Century

Monaco was reestablished in 1814 as a protectorate of the Kingdom of Sardinia by the Congress of Vienna in 1815, and remained like this until 1860 when the Treaty of Turin caused it to become ceded to France as a protectorate.

20th Century

In 1943, the Italian Army invaded Monaco to form a fascist administration. Shortly after the collapse of Mussolini, Monaco was then occupied by Nazi Germany until the end of World War II. Prince Rainier III took over as ruler of Monaco after the death of his grandfather, Prince Louis II in 1949.

In 1962, an amendment was passed in Monaco to abolish capital punishment, provide women's suffrage, and establish a Supreme of Monaco.

In 1963, Charles de Gaulle blockaded Monaco due to its well known status as a tax haven for wealthy Frenchmen.

In 1993, Monaco became a member of the United Nation, and was given full voting rights.

21st Century

In 2002, a treaty was signed that if there are no heirs to carry on the Grimaldi dynasty, the principiality of Monaco will remain an independent nation than revert to France.

After a a reign of 56 years, Rainier III died on April 6, 2005, leaving his son, Albert II, to take rule.

Geography

Location
