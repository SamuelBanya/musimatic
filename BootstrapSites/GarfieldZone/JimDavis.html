Early and Personal Life

  • Jim Davis was born in Marion, Indiana, and grew up on a farm in Fairmount, Indiana.
  • He lived on the farm during his childhood with a brother and 25 cats
  • This parallels the Garfield comic character, Jon Arbuckle, who also was raised on a farm.
  • Jim Davis attended Ball State University for a major in art and business.
  • He later joined the faculty as an adjunct professor in fall 2016.
  • Jim currently lives in Albany, Indiana, where he works with his staff to produce the Garfield comic under his Paws, Inc. company.

Career Highlights

  • 1969: Jim Davis began assisting Tom Ryan for the Tumbleweeds.
  • 1972: Jim Davis created the comic strip, Gnorm Gnat, which focused on a fictional gnat named Gnorm.
  • June 19, 1978: Garfield began syndication in 41 newspapers.
  • March 3, 1986: U.S. Acres begins syndication in 505 newspapers.
  • 2002: Garfield became the world's most syndicated comic strip, which appeared in 2,570 newspapers, and 263 million readers worldwide.
